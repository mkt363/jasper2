Free Ways To Get Keyword Ideas

Search Marketing Topics and Optimization

Did your free trial on those fancy keyword planning tools run out?

Are you a beginner to keyword research not wanting to spend a ton while you learn the concepts?

Do you just want be as cost-effective as possible?

Keywords are a crucial component to SEO.

Every good search marketing campaign usually starts with thorough keyword research.

But what happens when you run out of ideas and need new ways to get them?

Check out these unique ways to get more keyword ideas for free!

1. Related Searches

Ever notice that at the bottom of a search engine results page (SERP) there is a section usually titled “Searches related to ____”?

This section is your friend, my friends.

It contains a list of other searches people have done related to the keyword entered in the search bar.

Related searches is great, alternative way to gather key phrases.

2. Autocomplete

When you start typing into Google, it will start to auto suggest search queries based on common searches and what it thinks you are looking for.

From this auto suggested list, you can get a feel for popular phrases people search for.

This is a good place to start generating keyword ideas.

3. Incognito Mode

Search engines like Google are committed to showing the most relevant search results based on a user query.

Technology has advanced the point where Google can show different search results to different people depending on user preferences, activities, and search history.

Because of this, the keyword ideas you get from using related searches or autocomplete might be biased in your favor.

Exhibit A

See how when I just type “what is”, all of the possible search queries are related to SEO. That’s because Google knows I have been spending a lot of time on SEO related content lately.

To combat this, log out of all Google accounts and open an Incognito window.

Now repeat the above tips in Incognito mode to get true search suggestions.

4. Pencil and Paper

We’re taking it old school.

Sit down with pencil and a piece of paper and write down every keyword phrase or variation you can think of that comes to your head.

This is going to require a little imagination. If you get stuck and can’t think of any more, grab a thesaurus or ask a friend to help you grow your list.

5. Talk to Your Customers

Get keyword ideas straight from the minds of your intended customers by doing your research and talking directly to them.

Also, investigate what potential customers are searching for via forums like Quora or Reddit.

You can pick out keyword phrases straight from the questions, such as:
“Best digital marketing course”
“Basics of digital marketing”
“How to learn digital marketing”

6.Free Tools

Not all SEO tools cost money to benefit from.

While many tools can require users to connect their credit cards for access, there are other tools out there that are completely free or at least let you use a free version of the product.

Try using Google Keyword Planner or Ubersuggest next time you need some keyword ideas.

These tools take a base keyword, then auto-generate thousands of keyword ideas within seconds.

What is great about these tools is that they can tell you the competitiveness of a keyword, search volume, and can even let you target your results based on geographic location.


I’m sure in the future when you are a certified SEO Master you will have the funds to use the fancy, but costly, search tools available on the market.

Still, keep these methods in mind anytime you need to get creative!
